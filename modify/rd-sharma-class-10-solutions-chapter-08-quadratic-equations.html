Summary:

A polynomial of degree 2 is called a quadratic polynomial. The general form of a quadratic polynomial is ax 2 +bx+c, where a, b, c are real numbers such that a is not equal to 0 and x is a real variable.

If p(x)= ax 2 +bx+c, and a 0 is a quadratic polynomial and is a real number, then p()=a 2 +b+c is known as the value of the quadratic polynomial p(x)

A real number is said to be a zero of quadratic polynomial p(x) = ax 2 +bx+c, if p() =0.

If p(x) = ax 2 +bx+c is a quadratic polynomial, then p(x) = 0 i.e., ax 2 +bx+c =0, a 0 is called a quadratic polynomial.

A real number is said to be a root of the quadratic equation ax 2 +bx+c=0, if a 2 +b+c=0.
