Question 13.
The total surface area of a hollow metal cylinder, open at both ends of external radius 8 cm and height 10 cm is 338 π cm 2 . Taking r to be inner radius, obtain an equation in r and use it to obtain the thickness of the metal in the cylinder.
Solution:
Total surface area of a hollow metal cylinder = 338π cm 2
Let R be the outer radius, r be inner radius and h be the height of the cylinder of the cylinder
∴ 2πRh + 2πrh + 2πR 2 – 2πr 2 = 338π
R = 8 cm, h = 10 cm
⇒ 2πh (R + r) + 2π(R 2 – r 2 ) = 338π
⇒ Dividing by 2π , we get
⇒ h(R + r) + (R 2 – r 2 ) = 169
⇒ 10(8 + r) + (8 + r) (8 – r) = 169
⇒ 80 + 10r + 64 – r 2 = 169
⇒ 10r – r 2 + 144 – 169 = 0
⇒ r 2 – 10r + 25 = 0
⇒ (r-5) 2 = 0
⇒ r = 5
∴ Thickness of the metal = R – r = 8 – 5 = 3 cm
