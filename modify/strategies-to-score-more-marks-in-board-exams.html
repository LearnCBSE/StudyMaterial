5 Strategies to score more marks in Board Exams

February 25, 2016 by LearnCBSE Online

Strategies to score more marks in Board Exams

Every student appearing for the board exam is concerned about his or her score on the exam. The common question asked by every student is that how could they score more percentage, what are the tips and strategies that could help them to score high percentage.

Students come up with several questions in their mind. Well following some simple tricks and tips one can have a significant boost in their percentage. Some common question that every student is worried about his can everyone score percentage 90+? It is all about your hard work from the initial stage if you have done the needful preparation you can score more than 90 percent in your board exam.

Another frequently asked questions by the student are will their percentage effect due to the evaluation process? Yes, there is a chance of evaluation process affecting your marks it could be either in a good way or a bad way.

Some Tips to Score more in board exams:
However scoring high percentage depends on your hard work and preparation right from the beginning. Yet by following some simple steps one can score well in their exams.

Tip 1: Compete with yourself:
Have a look at your previous marks and get the thought of performing better than this in the next test. Work on improving your scores by taking as many tests and strive hard to beat your previous score. By doing these you would notice a gradual improvement in your scores.

Tip 2: Be thorough with your syllabus:
There are various reference books available in the market but instead of relying on these heavyweight books like RD Sharma , Lakhmir Singh it would be best if you stick to your syllabus book. The syllabus book is designed strictly according to the board syllabus and the questions in the exam would never come out of the syllabus. One can work on Chapterwise important questions from the syllabus.

Tip 3: Evaluate your weak points:
Being known about your weakness will help you to be better. Try to make your weak points strong while preparing for the exams. Go through all your test papers and preliminary papers. Find your mistakes and make a list for all the subjects. Check where you go wrong and make sure you are not repeating the mistake in your board exams. Dig to the scratch to convert your weak points into strong ones. See that you do not underestimate the easy or favorite subject, be thorough with every subject. Also, look at your handwriting. Deduction of even a single marks due to any reason would have an impact on your percentage.

Tip 4: Manage your time:
It is crucial that you do not neglect the time and manage it properly. Time management is an important factor and it can make a huge difference. Study the theory subjects in the morning as it helps to retain a long memory while reading in the morning. Do not waste time on unnecessary stuff, use free time in school to practice logical subjects. Self-study is a must. Divide your time for revision of each and every subject. While attending the board exam read the questions and appear the paper with the time management strategy so that you complete the paper on time. Do not spend more time on a particular question.

Tip 5: Solve previous year papers:
By solving the previous year paper you can get a clear view how your board exam paper will be. There is a chance of questions repeating from the previous paper. Solving previous paper will help you to know how the board exam will be. Boost your confidence by solving more previous year papers and CBSE Sample papers . By solving these papers you could identify the difficulty level. You can figure out how to secure your marks easily with these papers.

Things to do Night before exam and on the exam day:
Take rest and let your mind relax and be prepared for the final day. See that you have all the necessary stationery like pen, pencil, scale, rubber and stuff like that ready for the next day. Have good long sleep as it will reset your brain. In the morning have a quick revision of all the topics. Be confident and do not panic. Be calm and head to the examination center without any stress. All the best for your exams!!!
