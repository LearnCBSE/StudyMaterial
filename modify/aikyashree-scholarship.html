November 6, 2023 by LearnCBSE Online

Aikyashree Scholarship 2023-2024: Aikyashree Scholarships are intended for students who are citizens of the state at both the school and college levels. Operated by the government of West Bengal and its subsidiary boards, the scholarships for West Bengal strive at encouraging exemplary, worthy, and impoverished students of the state. Besides, the state also owns several reputed institutes of higher education like the Indian Institute of Management (Kolkata), Indian Institute of Technology (Kharagpur), Indian Institute of Science Education and Research (Kolkata), and National Institute of Technology (Durgapur) which offers the education for students of West Bengal more comfortable and hassle-free.

This section will give you complete information about the scholarships for Aikyashree, their eligibility, the application process, and the application period. Aikyashree Scholarship Details

Let us discuss here each scholarship provided by West Bengal state.

Swami Vivekananda Merit Cum Means Scholarship for Minorities, West Bengal: This is one of the known merit-cum-means based scholarships for pupils of West Bengal that includes education from class 11 onwards. Suitable only for minority community students, this scholarship gives financial support of up to Rs. 8000 per month depending on the program or level of education you are seeking. This scholarship is distributed to students regarding their academic advantage and financial need.

Bigyani Kanya Medha Britti Scholarship, West Bengal: This scholarship is mainly intended for girl students who have qualified for class 12th from any board and are currently seeking an undergraduate degree course in Basic Science/Medicine/Engineering at any institute of West Bengal. The purpose of this plan is to support girls’ higher education and maintain their scientific ability. The girl students staying in West Bengal are qualified to apply for this scholarship. The chosen students will get a whole of INR 3000 per month. In addition to this, the award also includes an annual book grant of INR 2000.

West Bengal Post-Matric Scholarship for SC/ST/OBC: Working under the Backward Classes Welfare Department, this scholarship for West Bengal students works to give financial support to SC/ST/OBC students studying at post-matriculation or post-secondary level to empower them to achieve their education. Students of class 11th, 12th, graduation, and post-graduation are qualified for this scholarship. The amount of scholarship that you can get under this plan may range from INR 160 to INR 1200.

West Bengal Pre-Matric Scholarship for SC ST Students: Performed by the Ministry of Social Justice and Empowerment, Government of India and administered by the Backward Classes Welfare Department, Government of West Bengal, this is a centrally sponsored Pre-Matric Scholarship Scheme for SC and ST scholars studying in class 9th and 10th. The scholarship allows variable financial support ranging from INR 150 per month to INR 750 per month. Also, the chosen scholars get an additional Adhoc reward of up to INR 1000 per annum.

Hindi Scholarship Scheme, West Bengal: To support and assist the study of Hindi language in Non-Hindi speaking states like West Bengal, this scholarship gives financial support to students studying in higher secondary, undergraduate, postgraduate and research level courses (M.Phil., Ph.D. or M Lit. etc). Students who have passed the Madhyamika, higher secondary, graduation, post-graduation or any other equivalent qualification can utilize for this scholarship to be a chance to win variable awards varying from INR 300 per month to INR 1000 per month.

Aikyashree Scholarship Eligibility Criteria

Though every scholarship has its set of pre-defined eligibility requirements, the critical condition to be qualified for West Bengal scholarships is that the candidate should be a permanent citizen of West Bengal state or be a domicile of the place. Besides this, there are various other criteria also that you require to follow concerning your educational qualification, academic merit, financial need, etc. These rules may vary from scholarship to scholarship.

Swami Vivekananda Merit Cum Means Scholarship for Minorities, West Bengal

  • Candidate must be a domicile of West Bengal.
  • If studying at the higher secondary level or diploma, the candidate must have secured at least 75% marks in class 10th.
  • If studying at the undergraduate level, the candidate must have secured at least 75% marks in class 12th.
  • If studying at PG level, the student must have secured at least 53% marks in graduation (for standard education) or 55% marks (in case of engineering)
  • The annual family assets should not exceed INR 2.5 Lakh.

Bigyani Kanya Medha Britti Scholarship, West Bengal

  • Candidate must be a girl student and a domicile of West Bengal.
  • Candidate should have qualified class 12th from any board and need to be studying Engineering, Medicine or Basic Science at a known institution in West Bengal.

West Bengal Post-Matric Scholarship for SC/ST/OBC

  • Candidate should be a domicile of West Bengal and belong to SC/ST/OBC category.
  • The student must be studying in Class 11, 12, Graduation or Postgraduation.
  • The family income of students should not exceed INR 2 Lakh to INR 2.5 Lakh per annum for SC/ST.
  • The family income should not exceed INR 1 Lakh per annum, for OBC students

West Bengal Pre-Matric Scholarship for SC/ST Students

  • Having a domicile of West Bengal is compulsory.
  • Candidate should belong to SC/ST category.
  • Candidate must be class 9 or 10, currently.
  • The annual income of the family should not exceed INR 2 Lakh per annum from all resources.

Hindi Scholarship Scheme, West Bengal

  • Candidate should belong to a non-Hindi-speaking state.
  • Candidate must be a citizen of West Bengal.
  • Candidate should have passed the Madhyamik, higher secondary, graduation, PG or any equivalent degree course.
  • Candidate must have secured at least 60% marks in the earlier qualifying exam in the first trial with Hindi as one of the subjects.

Aikyashree Scholarship 2023 Apply Online

Step – 1: Student Registration

  • Visit the official website of Aikyashree Scholarship @ http://wbmdfcscholarship.gov.in/
  • Click on the New Registration or Student Registration.
  • Now fill the student registration form by providing your name, District, Guardians Name, DOB, Mobile Number, Email id and Bank Account Number.
  • Now click on Submit and Procced.

Step – 2: Scheme Eligibility

Now students need to submit details like enter your Current Institution Name, Address, Percentage of Marks obtained in the Last Examination, Annual Family Income. Now click on submit.

Step – 3: Registration

After submitting all this information now students get the scholarship name for which you are eligible. students will get a Temporary user ID for your application. Students will get User ID and Password to their mail id.

Step – 4: Student’s Login

Now students need to login to the website by entering User ID and password, now fill the information under the ‘Basic information & Academic Information’ section. Now verify your bank account information.

Step – 5: Final Submit

Now preview your application form and all the details information you already provided. If all the information is correct then submit the application.

Step – 6: Take Print out & Submit for verification

After completing the student of the above steps takes a print out the computer-generated application form. Sign on it and attach the required documents with it. Then submit this application form to the head of your institution for further Aikyashree Scholarship verification.

FAQ’s on Aikyashree Scholarship

Question 1.
What is the objective of the Aikyashree scholarship?

Answer:
The main aim of the WBMDFC Scholarship Scheme is to support minority populations to send their children to school and to prevent dropout. This plan also encourages students to proceed with their higher education, improving their employability potential.

Question 2.
What are the WBMDFC Aikyashree Scholarship Schemes?

Answer:

  • WB Pre Matric Scholarship
  • WB Post Matric Scholarship
  • WB Merit cum Means Scholarship
  • WB Talent Support Scholarship
  • Swami Vivekananda Merit cum Means Scholarship (SVMCM)

Question 3.
What documents are required for minority scholarship?

Answer:

  • Passport size photo of the applicant.
  • Income certificate.
  • Self-certified community certificate.
  • Self-attested certificate of previous year’s mark sheet.
  • Fee receipt of the current course year.
  • Bank account details.
  • Residential/Domicile certificate.

Question 4.
What is the helpline number?

Answer:
Students can contact to the WBMDFC Helpline for Aikyashree Scholarship 2023 through this WhatsApp Number – 8017071714 or can call at 18001202130 (Toll-Free), 033-4047468 phone numbers.

Question 5.
What is Aikyshree Scholarship Last Date 2023?

Answer:
The Last date to apply for WBMDFC Aikyashree Scholarship is 31st October 2023 for Post Matric, MCM & TSP and November for SVMCM Scholarship.

Question 6.
Who can apply for Aikysahree Scholarship?

Answer:
Only Minority Students from the communities such as Muslim, Christian, Buddhist, Sikh, Jain, and Parsee can apply for this scholarship. Candidates must read in class One to the Ph.D. course.

Question 7.
What is the minimum Percentage required for Aikyashree Scholarship Scheme?

Answer:
For Pre-Matric, Post-Matric & Swami Vivekananda Merit Cum Means Scholarship, candidates must have to pass with minimum 50% marks on his/her last annual exam. For the Swami Vivekananda Merit cum Means (SVMCM) Scholarship scheme, candidates have to pass with 75% marks in Madhyamik or HS (for HS, Polytechnic, Honours), 53% mars in Honours (for Post Graduate course) and 55% marks in BE/B.Tech (for M.Tech Course). Below 50% marks for WBMDFC Tallent Support Scholarship scheme

Question 8.
What is the Aikyshree Scholarship Amount?

Answer:
The scholarship amount will vary course to course for Aikyashree Scheme. Rs 1,100 to 11,000 per year for Pre-matric Students (Class I to X). Rs 10,200 to 16,500 per year for Post-matric students (Class XI to Ph.D.). Rs 22,000 to 33,000 per year for Merit cum Means Scholarship (Technical and Professional course students). Rs 12,000 to 60,000 per year for Swami Vivekananda Merit cum Means Scholarship (SVMCM). Rs 2,500 to 4,900 per year under the Tallent Support Scholarship scheme.
