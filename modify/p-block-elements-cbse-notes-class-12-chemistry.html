II Group 16 Elements
9. In group 16 of the Periodic Table, elements, oxygen ( 8 O), sulphur ( 16 S), selenium ( 34 Se), tellurium ( 52 Te) and polonium ( 84 Po) are present.
10. The elements have the electronic configuration ns2np4 for their valence shells. The first element of the group 16 differs in its chemical behaviour from that of other members of the group due to its small size and high electronegativity.
11. The metallic character increases with increase in atomic number. The first four elements are non- metallic in character. Non-metallic character is strongest in O and S, weaker in Se and Te while Po is metallic.
12. Atomic and ionic radii increases from top to bottom, due to increase in the number of shells.
