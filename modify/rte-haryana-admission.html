RTE Haryana Admission 2019 | Application Form, Dates, Eligibility Criteria

May 10, 2020 by LearnCBSE Online

RTE Haryana Admission 2019: The right to compulsory and free education act or right to education act of 2009 is an act of parliament of India that was enacted on 4th August 2009. This act describes the modalities and importance of free and compulsory education for children between the age group of 6 and 14. This is under the 21a article of Indian constitution. Thereby, India became one of the 135 counties in the world to make education as a fundamental right to every child when this act came into place on 1st April 2010. In this article, we are going to provide you details about RTE Haryana admission 2019.

RTE Admission

This right is for the students that belong to BPL and EWS categories and are in class 9th to 12th (except for class 11) in private schools. This right was amended in 2013 under the rule 13A of Haryana school education rules. This right is for the academic session 2019-20 and candidates can go through this article to get more details related yo schedule for this year’s application process, RTE admission, eligibility, reporting time, age criteria, certificates required for selection and admission process.

RTE Haryana Admission 2019 Eligibility Criteria

The age of the applicant who is appearing for the admission process should be more than 6 years at the time of admission. Also, the age of the candidate at the time of admission process should not be more than 14 years.

FAQs on RTE Haryana

1.  Who can get Admission Under RTE?

Orphans, children with special needs, children of migrant workers and children of street workers are eligible for admission under the RTE Act.

2. How do I Process RTE Admission?

All the candidates can fill details correctly and can submit the completed application form to a 25% reservation for admission in govt.

3. Can I change School in RTE after getting Admission?

Where a child is required to move from one school to another, either within a state or outside, for any reason whatsoever, the child shall have the right to seek transfer to any other school, except private aided and special category school, for completing elementary education.

4. What is the income limit for admission under RTE?

A family whose earnings are Rs. 3.5 lakh or below can apply for seats under the RTE Act.
