Difference between NCERT and CBSE?

CBSE or Central Board of Secondary Education is a board which is the governing body while NCERT or National Council of Educational Research and Training is the counsel. NCERT is also known as the publishing body or publisher. CBSE schools in India recommends the NCERT textbooks to the students. In short, CBSE is a board whereas NCERT is a council.

What is NCERT and its functions?

The full form of NCERT is National Council of Educational Research and Training. It is an autonomous organisation set up in 1961 by the Government of India, aiming to assist and advise the Central as well as the State Governments on policies and programmes for qualitative improvement in school education. A few, but major, goal of NCERT are as follows:

  1. To gear up, encourage and correlate research in areas connected with school education
  2. To put together and publish textbooks, supplementary material, newsletters, journals, digital materials, and other study materials.
  3. To organise pre-service and in-service training of teachers along with developing and distributing innovative educational techniques and practices

The function of NCERT is to conduct and support educational research and to offer training in educational research methodology.

Is NCERT is the best resource for board exam ?

It is a widely known fact that NCERT books are the best for CBSE board exam preparation. But then again, simply byhearting the NCERT books a few weeks before the exams is not going to help the students with anything. Students should be thorough with the NCERT books well ahead of the board exam, giving them ample time to practice more questions from other reference books like RS Aggarwal, RD Sharma etc. Once done with studying and practicing with the books, students should solve the sample papers as well as previous year question papers to get an idea of the question paper pattern.

What is NCERT Exemplar and where can we get its Solutions?

NCERT Exemplars are basically practice-books that contain additional questions of a higher level, apart from NCERT textbooks, and are meant for providing comprehensive and thorough learning. The NCERT exemplar books accommodate numerous conceptual sums, which cover not only CBSE board exams, but also the competitive exams like JEE Mains and JEE advanced.
