Devaraj Arasu Scholarship Eligibility

All the candidates can who satisfy the Eligibility Criteria can easily submit their application form via the official portal of the Devaraj Asaru Scholarship. Check the Eligibility Guidelines here given below:

  • Applicants should be Permanent residents of Karnataka.
  • Applicants should be fall under the category list of Backward Classes released by the government of Karnataka.
  • Applicants should be pursuing education from lawful universities / subordinate / government / local bodies / subsidiaries / recognized non-subsidized private institutions.
  • The overall family Income should not be more than INR 2.50 lakh.

About Department of Backward Classes Welfare

The Government of Karnataka created a separate “Backward Classes and Minority Department” for the welfare of the backward classes, minorities in the state in 1977 and provided for the provision of educational reservation under Article 15 (4) of the Constitution of India and 16 (4). In 1999-2000, the Minority Welfare Department had a separate Directorate, separate from the Backward Classes Welfare Department.

FAQ’s on Devaraj Arasu Scholarship 2021

Question 1.
What is the Devaraja Arasu Scholarship?

Answer:
Devaraja Arasu Pratibha Award 2021 is a scholarship granted by the Department of Backward Classes Welfare, Government of Karnataka to the extraordinary scoring students of SSLC/Secondary PUC classes who belong to backward classes. It gives benefits of up to INR 15,000.

Question 2.
Who is proposing this scholarship?

Answer:
Department of Backward Classes Welfare, Government of Karnataka is presenting this scholarship to students from the backward classes.

Question 3.
What are the benefits given under Devaraj Arasu Scholarship?

Answer:
The selected candidates will get multiple awards up to INR 15,000.

Question 4.
Who can apply for this scholarship?

Answer:
To be qualified, a candidate must:

  • Be a resident of Karnataka state
  • Have attended the SSLC or secondary PUC for the year 2019-20
  • Have scored 90% or above in the annual examination from the recognized institute/school
  • Belong to backward classes specified by the government of the Karnataka
