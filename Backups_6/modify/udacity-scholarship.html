Edge Development with Intel: This scholarship program declared at the Intel AI Summit and the Future of Education and Workforce Summit in San Francisco will encourage professional developers involved in advanced learning, especially in-depth knowledge and computer vision, to stimulate the improvement and deployment of high-performance computer vision and comprehensive learning solutions.

Computer vision and AI at the edge are growing instrumental in powering everything from factory assembly lines and retail inventory administration to hospital urgent care medical imaging devices like X-ray and CAT scans. Bertelsmann Tech Scholarship: Bertelsmann is financing 15,000 scholarships in Data, AI, and Cloud-Computing in connection with Udacity, and has announced that applications are presently open. Secure and Private AI Challenge Scholarship from Facebook: This scholarship program published at F8, the Facebook Developer Conference in San Jose, will allow students to gain abilities in Federated Learning, Differential Privacy, and Encrypted Computation with the advantage of substantial alliance help from Udacity.You will discover how to use the latest privacy-preserving technologies, such as OpenMined’s PySyft.

PySyft elongates PyTorch and other deep learning means with the cryptographic and shared techniques needed to safely and securely train AI models on categorized private data while managing users’ privacy. Students will also have the occasion to get access to a full scholarship to either the Deep Learning Nanodegree program or the Computer Vision Nanodegree program with Udacity.

This program has two stages:

  • Phase 1: 5,000 call scholarships to a scholarship request course based on Facebook’s newly published Secure and Private AI course with Udacity. This challenging course will last for three months, and program participants will get assistance from community managers for the term of the program. They will be a member of a vibrant student community.
  • Phase 2: 300 top-performing students from Phase 1, who exhibit leadership and dedication to practicing secure and private AI for the public good, will go on to receive full scholarships for either the Deep Learning or Computer Vision Nanodegree programs.

Udacity KPIT Autonomous Tech Scholarship

Udacity and KPIT have built a new scholarship for ambitious independent vehicle engineers in India. Successful candidates will start Udacity’s Self-Driving Car Engineer Nanodegree program, and the best performers will get a job interview with KPIT. Udacity has declared the Autonomous Tech Scholarship, a new trial with global technology firm KPIT. They have partnered with KPIT to give scholarship shots to citizens of India who are excited to join the world of self-governing technology and develop their professions in this transformational area. The scholarship experience is divided into two phases:

Scholarship Phase 1

All applications will be reviewed thoroughly, following which they will choose around 500 winners to start Term 1 of Udacity’s Self-Driving Car Engineer Nanodegree program. Registered students will later have two months to complete Term 1 successfully. Throughout your studies, you will examine theories such as computer vision and sensor fusion, and discover how to implement deep learning to automotive difficulties. You will use these newly-learned methods in real-world outlines centered on topics such as the discovery of lane lines, the prophecy of steering wheels angles, and more.

Scholarship Phase 2

The top 100 performers in Term 1 of the Self-Driving Car Engineer Nanodegree program will get the occasion to examine with KPIT for global job opportunities. Each candidate who successfully interviews and gets employed will additionally get free registration into Term 2 of the Self-Driving Car Engineer Nanodegree program, to assure you are able to resume your studies and make the most of your new job opportunity.

What is the “Bertelsmann Technology Scholarship Program”?

Answer:
Bertelsmann is launching a global education initiative to strengthen people’s tech skills: over a three-year period, Bertelsmann is investing several million euros in funding as many as 50,000 tech scholarships on the online education platform Udacity. Who is eligible to apply for a pledge to America’s workers’ scholarship?

Answer:
To receive one of these scholarships, you must be a U.S. citizen or permanent U.S. resident with a valid work permit. You must also be at least 18 years old and complete the application in full.
