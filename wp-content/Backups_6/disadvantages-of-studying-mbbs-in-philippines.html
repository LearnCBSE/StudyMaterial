12 Major Disadvantages of Studying MBBS in Philippines – MBBS Abroad

September 6, 2023 by LearnCBSE Online

Disadvantages of Studying MBBS in Philippines: Completed your NEET Exam and interested in going to the Philippines for an MBBS degree? But before you go to the Philippines for medical study you need to know about the pros as well as cons of studying MBBS in the Phillippines.

You may think that Phillippines is a rich country, and it has blossomed into a medical education hotspot with so many elite universities. So, there may not be any disadvantages to know about, but that is not true. For every country, there are pros and cons to studying MBBS.

So, let us look into the article and check about the disadvantages of Studying MBBS in the Philippines.

Busting 12 Disadvantages of Studying MBBS in the Philippines

The Philippines is a great country to study MBBS but still, there are some things that people need to know as they are not so good. As you may already have researched about good things, knowing about bad things is also important. And then you can decide whether to go to that country to study MBBS or not.

Below are the major disadvantages of Studying MBBS in the Philippines.

  1. Climatic Conditions
  2. Unwanted Addiction of Modern Culture
  3. Food and Lodging
  4. NMAT Philippines
  5. Pre Medical Course or BS Degree
  6. Medical Council of India Screening Test
  7. Eligibility Certificate from the Medical Council of India (MCI)
  8. Less Clinical Rotations
  9. High Cost of Living
  10. Language
  11. VISA Process and Fee
  12. Internship Training

Climatic Conditions

The main disadvantage of Studying MBBS in the Philippines is its climatic conditions. As the Philippines is an island it has more rainy seasons compared to India. It starts around June and lasts till November.

Even there are high chances of heavy cyclones which affects very badly. So if you are the one who is unable to set for any climatic conditions then you will face many challenging issues that even make you sick.

Unwanted Addiction of Modern Culture

One more disadvantage that comes next is the culture in the Philippines. Philippine culture is majorly affected and influenced by the cultures of Spain and the United States. That influence is nothing but the exposure of nightclubs.

Once students start distracting from the night clubs it will become addictive to them and even affect the health of the students. Even these nightclubs have other illegal practices like harmful drugs and alcohol.

Food and Lodging

Every one of us knows that when we go abroad food and lodging will be the major issue. And when it comes to the Philippines, here it is even worse as most students need to live in PG or rent accommodations near their college. Even students need to pay their meals and other expenses on their own.

Unlike in other countries, students cannot able to stay in government hostels. And some colleges do not have any hostel facilities. And even finding an excellent meal is a problem for international students. And it may lead to health issues too.

NMAT Philippines

NMAT is an entrance exam that is required to study MBBS in the Philippines. This test can be written while you are in the BS course itself as there will be 1- 4 chances to complete this exam. And this exam is not that difficult if you have already written the NEET exam.

The qualifying mark for this NMAT exam is 40. And if you fail this exam, he/she is unable to pass the licensing test in their country. But we have many colleges who will admit you into MD courses even if you fail in this exam.

However, you need to pass this NMAT exam with 40 marks, so you will get 2- 4 chances additionally.

Pre Medical Course or BS Degree

Look into one more disadvantage to studying MBBS in the Philippines is about education system. As education in the Philippines is different from the Indian education system, Indian students who are willing to study MBBS in the Philippines need to enroll for this BS degree or Pre-Medical Course and it is mandatory.

In this BS degree, you will have the subjects of pharmacy, biology, psychology, and Medical technology. biochemistry and microbiology. After completion of 12th and NEET, you need to study BS degree which is of 2 year course.

Once you successfully complete a BS degree and qualify for NMAT then you will get a chance to study MBBS. It is time time-consuming process.

Medical Council of India Screening Test

Another disadvantage of Studying MBBS in the Philippines is the MCI screening test. Indian students who completed their graduation abroad should definetly take a screening test called FMGE ( Foreign Medical Graduates Exam).

That exam will be conducted by the Medical Council of India. By qualifying in this exam you will get a chance to practice in India. This screening test will be conducted twice. If you fail, you need to pass the test and start practice.

Eligibility Certificate from the Medical Council of India (MCI)

When you are interested in studying MBBS in the Philippines, there is a disadvantage in choosing colleges, that is students need to choose the college list from the Medical Council of India.

And even if you want to study in the Philippines you need to get the eligibility certificate from the Medical Council of India that is issued by the government.

Less Clinical Rotations

When you are studying MBBS in India or in another country there will be a lot of lab sessions that the students need to perform in clinics. But coming to the Philippines.

First three years you are unable to go to any lab sessions, only in the fourth year you will be allowed to perform in clinics. So that students will not get much experience. And even that fourth-year clinical trials will also not be allowed in some areas of the Philippines.

Finally as a result students are less likely to be exposed to patients and diseases in real life.

High Cost of Living

The major disadvantage more than anything else is the high cost of living in the Philippines. As they do not provide any hostel facilities in colleges, students need to take separate rooms, and flats, and live as paying guests. So expenses will be higher in cost than hostels.

Even students need to pay for their food, rooms, college, and other expenditures separately. It will be very difficult for the students to arrange money.

Language

Language is also the most common disadvantage for international students even though they speak English it is not so good and the accents are also not good as it leads to difficulties for the students to understand.

Students should even need to learn the local language as an additional subject.

VISA Process and Fee

If you are thinking of studying MBBS abroad then you must first go through the VISA and interview process Based on the interview that the student has given the VISA will be provided. When you go to the Philippines, the same VISA will be converted to a student VISA.

And the Visa needs to be renewed every year by paying the renewal charges.

Internship Training

As we have seen many disadvantages till now, internship training is also a disadvantage. Because students Who studied MBBS in the Philippines are not allowed to touch the patients in internship training. They need to just see them while the main doctors are doing it.

Internship training is completely done on dummies only as they are not allowed to touch the patients. Due to this for intern students, there will be a lack of real-time experience.

FAQs on Disadvantages of Studying MBBS in the Philippines

  1. Is it worth studying MBBS in the Philippines?

Yes, it is worth studying MBBS in the Philippines as it is the best place to study MBBS at a reasonable price for its students.

  1. What is the major disadvantage of studying MBBS in the Philippines?

The major disadvantage of studying MBBS in the Philippines is Cost of living.

  1. Is the Philippines better than India for MBBS?

Yes, studying MBBS in the Philippines is better than studying MBBS in India.
