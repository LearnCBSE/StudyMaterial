What is Education Loan? – How Does Education Loan Work? – Process, Interest Rates, Eligibility

August 8, 2023 by LearnCBSE Online

What is Education Loan?: Students who are passionate about their education but economically backward can avail the support to study & fulfill their dreams with the help of education loans.

Wondering what an education loan is and how to choose the correct study loan for you, jump into this guide. Also, you’ve covered additional details on education loans like eligibility, features, tax benefits, types of loans, list of offered banks, interest rates, etc.

What Is Education Loan?

Education loan is an outstanding scheme that supports passionate students to complete their academic careers. Under this scheme, a student can borrow a sufficient amount of money from the vendor or bank to pay expenses for post-secondary education or higher education.

Applicants can get benefits like tuition fees, supplies, books cost, and accommodation expenses while studying their course with the help of an education loan. There are various types of education loans and different vendors to support financially backward students and orphan kids.

Explore how does study loan works, who can avail of it, and how to apply by referring to the following modules.

How Does Study Loan Works?

Education loan works based on the selected type of study loan from the government or private lenders. Availing student loans means getting financial help to pursue a degree at a reputed college or university.

Government-provided loans come in low-interest rates and a few subsidized interest. Private lenders provided loans come with a lot of lending processes with higher rates than government education loans.

You can even learn different study loan types based on several factors. Find more details on the same below.

Types of Education Loans Based On Location, Course & Collateral

Mainly education loan is divided into two types. They are federal education loans and private education loans. Federal education loan means a loan offered by the federal government and private study loans mean a loan offered by banks or other financial institutions.

The following are some other types of student loans that are provided on the basis of various factors:

Based On Location

Domestic Education Loan: This loan is specifically for those students who want to study their course in India. If the applicant is having the Indian admission form then only he/she is eligible to apply for the education loan under this type.

Overseas Education Loan or Study Abroad Education Loan: Students who want to pursue their desired course in a foreign institution, should apply for an education loan under this type. They can be offered airfare, accommodation, tuition fee, and other benefits to study abroad after fulfilling all the eligibility criteria.

Check the guide on which bank gives education loans without collateral for abroad and gain some knowledge.

Based On Collateral

Third-Party Guarantee: A bank employee or a home bank should provide the guarantee letter for sanctioning the education loan to the student.

Loan Against Property, Deposits, and Securities: To get a loan for pursuing your studies, you should provide a guarantee by agricultural land, residential land, flat, house, and others, fixed deposit certificates, recurring deposits, gold deposits, bonds, debentures, and equity shares.

Based On Course

Undergraduate (UG) Loans: Undergraduate Loans are another type of education loan where students get financial help to pursue their UG degrees.

Postgraduate (PG) Loans: Students who got finished their UG Courses and desired to pursue their postgraduate program in India can apply for a study under this type.

Career Development Loans: Applicants who are working hard in corporate jobs, desired to level up their skills, and reach the top in their career can take help from this loan. With this loan type, they can pause their job and continue to pick up professional courses and training for better employment opportunities.

Kids or Graduated students who want to pursue their dream courses worldwide must be aware of the courses covered under an education loan before applying. So, we have curated a list of courses here for your reference, simple have an eye on them:

Education Loan Eligibility Criteria

Knowing complete knowledge of what makes you eligible for an education loan is very important. So, here we have provided some information on the same for quick reference on what government vendors or banks, or other private vendors ask you to sanction the study loan:

  • Applicants should be Indian Residents.
  • Get an Admission application form from the university in India or Abroad, where you want to study your dream course.
  • If you need a loan for the undergraduate program, you should complete a 10+2 education with a pass percentage.
  • For postgraduate courses, one must finish an undergraduate course.
  • Candidates’ age should fall between 18 to 35 years at that time.
  • Those who want to pursue the full-time course must have a co-applicant and have a normal income source.
  • Should hold all the below-mentioned required documents.

How to Apply for an Education Loan Online & Offline?

Students can apply for a study loan in various ways. Want to learn how to apply for it then check below:

1st: Offline Application Process

  • First choose the lender that you want to apply for a loan.
  • If you choose a bank, get the types of education loan details from the nearest branch. Choose a suitable loan type and fill out the application form. Along with that, know the documents required for submission and complete the process in person.
  • If you choose another lender, discuss the details by visiting their branch, or else call the lender and discuss the loan types, terms, and application process.

2nd: Online Application Process

  • One of the most convenient methods to apply for an education loan is by applying online. You can apply to study loan online in two ways.
  • Initially, the simple way is to download the application form online from valid sources or at the official website.
  • Fill out the loam form and submit it by attaching the other required papers too.
  • Second method is to apply by providing the requested information and then the lender will directly come to you for discussing the loan terms before applying it.

Tax Benefits on Education Loans

The following points will help you know what tax benefits you can get by availing education loan for pursuing a full-time or part-time course at a recognized institute. Here’s the information on the same:

  • The benefits provided by Section 80E of the Income Tax Act are tax deductions on the amount of interest paid toward education loans.
  • All types of study loans are eligible under this section but make sure you avail of the loan from a bank or a registered financial institution.
  • You won’t see any limitation on the deducted interest rate which is the best aspect of this tax benefit.
  • By submitting Form 16B or Form 16C along with ITR, you can easily claim the IT benefit in which you have paid the principal amount and interest component.
  • Applicants can avail of this benefit for 8 years from the start of loan repayment day or until the interest component is paid off, whichever arrives sooner.

FAQs On What Is Student Loan?

  1. What are the courses covered under an education loan?

The brief courses list that are accepted to cover under an education loan is as follows:

  • Undergraduate degrees
  • Undergraduate diplomas
  • Postgraduate degrees
  • Postgraduate diplomas
  • Vocational training courses
  • Doctoral courses
  • Skill development courses
  1. What do the loaners aim to approve the study loan?

Loaners set some of the factors as per their rules and they consider to fulfill all those factors during the approval of the loan. They are listed below:

  • Selected courses and institute
  • The required amount to study the chosen course
  • Your recent or past academic performance
  • Family property
  • Annual revenue
  • Repayment ability of your family
  1. Are NRIs eligible for education loans from banks in India?

No, NRIs are not eligible for education loans from banks in India. Applicants who are residents of India and who have given collateral or other documents for security must be enforceable in India.

  1. Does Education Loans from NBFCs & FinTech Companies Qualify for Income Tax Deductions?

No, Education loans from NBFCs & FinTech Companies do not qualify for Income Tax Deductions.

  1. Who can avail and apply for an education loan?

Any kid or student in India is eligible to apply for an education loan as a main borrower. The co-applicants for study loans are the parent, sibling, spouse, or guardian.

  1. What is the moratorium period for education loans?

The period of your course duration plus 6-12 months is the answer to what is moratorium period in education loan . For a better understanding of the moratorium period click on this link.

  1. What are the four major types of educational loans?

The four major types of federal educational loans available are Direct Subsidized Loans, Direct Unsubsidized Loans, Direct Consolidation Loans, and Direct PLUS Loans.
