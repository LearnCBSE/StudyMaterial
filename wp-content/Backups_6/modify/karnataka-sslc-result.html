Karnataka SSLC Results 2024 – Steps to Check

Karnataka Board Result 2024 for SSLC will be declared on the official website and through online mode only. Later, students can collect their SSLC mark sheet from their respective Karnataka Board affiliated schools Below here are the steps to check the results through online mode, for SSC class exam conducted this year in 2024.

  • Firstly, the candidate has to visit the official Karnataka board official results website, www.karresults.nic.in.
  • Click on the link given for Karnataka Board SSLC results in 2024.
  • A new webpage will get opened where you will be asked to enter the registration number.
  • Enter the number and click on submit button.
  • The result of SSLC Karnataka board exam will be displayed on your screen.
  • Download the result for your reference.

Students who have appeared for the Karnataka Board SSLC exam in 2024, have to score 30% in each subject and 35% in overall subjects to pass the examination. The class for the results is determined with respect to below-given criteria’s.

Percentage Class
50% – 59.99% Second Class
60% – 84.99% First Class
85% and above Distinction

Karnataka SSLC Results – Re-evaluation

If the students are not satisfied with their marks or if they have not passed the exam, they can choose for revaluation of their results, declared by the Karnataka board or KSEEB. To apply for it, students will have to download the application form for a re-evaluation of marks available in the Karnataka Board online website.

Students have to send the filled scrutiny form along with a nominal fee deposit fee to the Karnataka Board authority. After the submission of the form, the Karnataka board personnel will recheck the answer sheets for the subject, which has to be re-evaluated or the objection has been raised. The cross-checked results will be published again on the official Karnataka Board results site.

About KSEEB

KSEEB, Karnataka Secondary Education Examination Board was established in the year 1966. The aim of the Board is to restructure the operations of Secondary and PUC Education in Karnataka. The main objective is to conduct the board exam for SSLC classes and for PUC 1st year and 2nd year. Also, to prescribe syllabus for all the schools, private or government, which are affiliated to the board. The board is responsible to affiliate the private and government schools in Karnataka, also.

Karnataka board is responsible for the improvement of secondary education so that it meets the required needs and standards of economic and social development. KSEEB provides adequate facilities for the educational and professional advancement of socially and educationally backward communities.
