Question 13:
How many terms of G.P. 3, 3 2 , 3 3 , …………… are needed to give the sum 120?
Ans:
The given G.P. is 3, 3 2 , 3 3 , …………
Let n terms of this G.P. be required to obtain the sum as 120.
S n = \(\frac{a\left(r^{n}-1\right)}{r-1}\)
Here, a = 3 and r = 3
S n = 120 = \(\frac{3\left(3^{n}-1\right)}{3-1}\)

⇒ 120 = \(\frac{3\left(3^{n}-1\right)}{3-1}\)
⇒ \(\frac{120 \times 2}{3}\) = 3 n – 1
⇒ 3 n – 1 = 80
⇒ 3 n = 81
⇒ 3 n = 3 4
∴ n = 4
Thus, four terms of the given G.P. are required to obtain the sum as 120.

Question 20:
Show that the products of the corresponding terms of the sequences a, ar, ar 2 , … ar n – 1 and A, AR, AR 2 , …….. AR n – 1 form a G.P. and find the common ratio.
Ans:

It has to be proved that the sequence, aA, arAR, ar 2 AR 2 , ………. ar n – 1 AR n – 1 , forms a G.P.
\(\frac{\text { Second term }}{\text { First term }}=\frac{a r A R}{a A}\) = rR

\(\frac{\text { Third term }}{\text { Second term }}=\frac{a r^{2} A R^{2}}{a r A R}\) = rR
Thus, the above sequence forms a G.P. and the common ratio is rR.
