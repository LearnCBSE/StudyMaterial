NEET MBBS Counselling Tamil Nadu 2019 | Detailed Counselling Procedure, Dates, Seats and Fee

July 1, 2019 by LearnCBSE Online

NEET MBBS Counselling Tamil Nadu 2019: Tamil Nadu MBBS Counselling for medical and dental seats under 85% state quota through NEET 2019 will conduct by Directorate of Medical Education (DME), Tamil Nadu. Application Process for registration to NEET Tamil Nadu MBBS Counselling has been ended on June 20, 2019. DME will release NEET Rank List based on the marks submitted while application process. There are 3947 MBBS and 985 BDS Seats offered by the government and private colleges of Tamil Nadu and DME will conduct two rounds of counseling and mop-up round for admission. Those students whose name are in the state merit List will be called for seat allotment procedure.

NEET Tamil Nadu MBBS Counselling  2019 – Eligibility Criteria

  • Students who have qualified NEET 2019 by gaining minimum cutoff
  • The aspirant must be an Indian Citizen or an Overseas Citizens of India with a certificate proving their nationality.
  • Candidate must be a native of Tamil Nadu with a valid nativity certificate for verification at later stages.
  • Student’s age must be in between 17-25 as on May 5, 2019
  • Candidates must have cleared all the subjects in Class 12 with a minimum percentage of 50 for general category candidates and 40-45% for reserved and PH candidates.

NEET MBBS Counselling Tamil Nadu 2019 – Application Procedure.

The Tamil Nadu NEET MBBS application process is the first step in the counseling process. In this process, candidates must register on DME, Tamilnadu’s official website at tnhealth.org or tnmedicalselection.org. In the registration process, candidates are equipped with a photograph and signature along with with their personal information, NEET 2019 test score, residence status, category, educational details. It is also important that candidates provide valid communication details for further communication through the DME. After filling and submitting the details, the candidates should take the NEET MBBS Tamil Nadu Application Form printout and send it to the Post Office.

Along with the completed application form, candidates must also send the photocopies of certain documents to the following address:

Address: The Secretary, Selection Committee, 162, Periyar EVR High Road, Kilpauk, Chennai-600 010

Candidates must send the photocopies of the documents such as:

  • NEET- UG 2019 Admit card and Scorecard.
  • Class X Mark Sheet (both sides).
  • Class XI Mark Sheet (both sides).
  • H.S.C. Mark sheet (both sides) or any other equivalent examination.
  • Transfer certificate obtained after the completion of H.S.C. or equivalent courses.
  • Certificate for proof of study from VI Std. to XII Std.
  • Nativity certificate obtained from Tamil Nadu for the candidates who are seeking admission under minority category.
  • Permanent Community Certificate Card.

Note: Student should not send the original documents to the address given above as DME will not take any responsibility for loss of certificates.

In addition to the above-mentioned documents, candidates must also make a payment either through online mode or by sending a demand draft to the above address through post along with the application form. If the candidate chooses to make the payment through online then he or she must use Debit/Credit/ Net banking payment options.

NEET MBBS Counselling Tamil Nadu 2019 – Reporting and Seat Allotment

Based on the NEET Scores filled while application process, DME will prepare a merit list and release it by 2nd July 2019. Students whose name in the merit list will be called for counseling procedures. The call letters for NEET MBBS Counselling Tamil Nadu 2019 can be downloaded by logging in on the official website using the login id and password generated at the time of registration.

DME will conduct NEET MBBS Counselling Tamil Nadu in Offline mode. As per the instructions on the call letter, students must report at reporting (Nodal) centers for document verification (Original). Based on the vacant seats from the first round of counseling procedure, candidates will be called for the second round. If time permits, DME will also call the candidates as per their rank for the mop-up round. But if the candidate is absent for the 1st phase of Counselling, then he/she will not be permitted to attend the subsequent phase of Counselling. On successful completion of each round of counseling procedure, DME will release the seat allotment result on its official website. Based on the seat allotment result, candidates will be provided admissions in the colleges.
