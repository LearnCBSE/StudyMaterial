STS Scholarship 2024 | Application (Closes Soon), Eligibility, Selection, Rewards

December 2, 2023 by LearnCBSE Online

STS Scholarship 2024: The applications for STS Scholarship are invited from interested MBBS/BDS students, On behalf of Prof. Balram Bhargava, Director General, Indian Council of Medical Research and Secretary, Department of Health Research. STS stands here for Short Term Studentship.

Note:

  • Results will be declared in April and the list of elected student’s names presented on the website.
  • If chosen, the student is demanded to accomplish the project in any two given months between April and September each year and present the report before the last date of submission.
  • The students will be granted a stipend and certificate only if their report is approved.
  • The last date of registration and submission of the proposal each year is during the second half of January.
  • The choice of the applicants for the award of research studentship will be done after the technical evaluation of the research plan by a committee of experts.
  • The arrangement of ICMR in regard to the selection of students will be final.
  • Appeals for reconsideration will not be considered and reasons for refusal of applications for the award of the studentship will not be provided.

  • The guide must take overall responsibility for the conduct of the research project, preparation, and submission of the complete report & the required enclosures within the stipulated time period.
  • The student should get permission from the Institutional Ethics Committee (IEC) if the program involves research on human participants and from Institutional Animal Ethics Committee (IAEC) if the work involves the use of animals.
  • The IEC/ IAEC permission should be received any time between January–April before starting actual research work.
  • On approval of the STS Report, the stipend will be paid through RTGS and will be transferred to the student’s own single holder nationalized bank account number. Joint Accounts (parents/guides) will not be accepted.
  • The certificate may be presented either along with the project or with the report. Informed consent is to be collected for research on human participants. For more details please refer to ICMR National Ethical Guidelines for Biomedical and Health Research on Human Participants, 2017 and National Ethical Guidelines for Biomedical Research Involving Children respectively.

Question 1.
When do the STS scholarship registrations begin?

Answer:
The STS registration begins every year around 10th December and continues for one month.

Question 2.
How do I choose the topic for my research?

Answer:
Students are free to choose any topic from the list of topics given in the application process depending on their interest related to medical research. The topic should be relevant and should lead to the creation of new knowledge or help in gaining new knowledge or skills by the student. Research should not be carried out on well established/known facts.

Question 3.
How do I apply online for the STS scholarship?

Answer:
Please visit the ICMR website (www.icmr.nic.in) and go through all the information/ instructions under the STS link, register your email address and then apply online.

Question 4.
What are the eligibility criteria to apply for STS Scholarship?

Answer:
STS program is open only to undergraduate medical students (MBBS/BDS) who have not yet appeared in their final exams. Paramedical/dental/non-medical/students of other courses are not eligible.

Question 5.
What is the last date for registration/proposal/report submission?

Answer:
Information regarding the last date for registration and proposal/report submission is provided on the ICMR website. Kindly note that the last date will not be extended further.

Question 6.
What information is needed at the time of registration?

Answer:
Registration form is divided into three sections

  • Login Details: Student’s active email id and mobile number.
  • Course Details: Student name, course, college name and address.
  • Personal Details: Student’s date of birth and residence address.

medical

Question 7.
When will I receive my stipend?

Answer:
About 3 months (around May or June) after report results are declared stipend will be transferred through online mechanism into the student’s bank account as per details provided in the online submission form. An email will also be sent to inform the students at that time.
