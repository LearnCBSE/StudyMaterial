Eligibility Criteria For MBBS Abroad Indian Students

Who are aspiring to study MBBS Abroad need to meet the eligibility requirements. We will look into that criteria list below.

  • Candidate must have completed 10 + 2 from the recognized university with 50% aggregate in PCB subjects. For SC, ST, and OBC the aggregate score will be 40%.
  • Aspirant should be at least 17+ years of age, on or before 31st December of the year.
  • Candidate must have studied compulsory subjects like biology, physics, and chemistry, in the 12th grade.
  • Need proof of country language like IELTS and TOFEL.
  • NEET Entrance test scorecard is compulsory.

Disadvantages of Studying MBBS Abroad

Even though there are a lot of benefits that are available by studying MBBS abroad, there are also some disadvantages that you need to know before you think about studying MBBS Abroad.

  • If you want to practice in India after completion of MBBS abroad you need to pass the FMGE exam.
  • Climatic conditions will be different
  • You need to clear IELTS and TOEFL to study in some abroad countries.
  • Students may feel homesick
  • Differences in culture and traditions.
  • Some students face VISA process problems
  • Even though the place is safe to stay, some kind of fear will dupe them.
  • Language barrier is the most common disadvantage when you want to study MBBS abroad.
