NCERT Textbook Pages 149-150
1. Rahmat needs to find the length of the boundary of the field.

Can you find it from this picture? See the length of each side
written near it.
Ans. Length of the boundary
= 9 metre + 15 metre + 21 metre + 9 metre = 54 metre

2. Rahmat bought a roll of 70 m wire for the fence.
Ganpat-Can you give me the wire that is left with you? I will use it for my field. How much wire did Rahmat give to Ganpat?
Ans. The total length of wire = 70 m
Wire used by Rahmat for boundary = 54 m
Wire left = 70 m – 54 m = 16 m
So, Rahmat gave 16 m wire to Ganpat.

3. Ganpat thanked Rahmat and started fencing his own field.

But he needed to get more wire.
(а) How long is the boundary of Ganpat’s field?
Ans. The length of boundary of Ganpat’s field
= 9m + 15m + 15m + 9m+18m = 66 m
(b) How much more wire will Ganpat need for his field?
Ans. Length of the Boundary of Ganpat’s field = 66 m
Rahmat gave 16 m wire to Ganpat Need of the more length of wire = 66m-16m = 50m Ganpat needs 50 m more wire to boundary his field.

NCERT Textbook Page 152
1. Chandu’s father is called the ‘yung old man’ in his village. At 70 years of age, he is fully fit. Do you know his secret? He goes for a walk around the field every morning. Everyday he takes four rounds of Chandu’s field.
What is the total distance he covers?
4 x_______=_______m =_______ km

Ans. The boundary of the field = 150 m + 100 m + 150 m + 100 m = 500 m
The distance covered by young old man = 4 x 500 m = 2000 m = 2 km
Ganpat’s wife works in a tailor’s shop. She has to fix lace around a table cloth.
She bought a 100 metre roll of lace.
(a) Look at the picture of the table cloth and tell how much lace is used for one table cloth.

Ans. 1 m 50 cm = 100 cm + 50 cm = 150 cm
The length of lace required for one table cloth
= 150 cm + 50 cm + 150 cm + 50 cm .
= 400 cm = 4 metre
(b) How much lace will be used in 3 such table cloths?
Ans. For 1 table cloth requirement of lace = 4m
Therefore, for 3 table cloth requirement of lace = 4m x 3 = 12m
(c) How much lace will be left in the roll?
Ans. Total length of lace = 100m
Lace used to make 3 table cloths = 12m
Lace left after making of 3 table cloths = 100m – 12m = 88m

Practice Time
1. A square has a boundary of 12 cm.

(a) From the corner of this square, a small square of side 1 cm is cut off. Will the boundary of B be less or more? Find its length.
Ans. The boundary of shape ‘B’ is equal to the boundary of shape ‘C’
The boundary of ‘B’ = 12 cm.
(b) If you cut a 1 cm square to get shape C, what will be the length of the boundary of C?
Ans. The boundary of shape ‘C’ will be equals to 14 cm.

2. (a) Find the length of the boundary of square D.

Ans. The boundary of shape D = 5 cm + 5 cm + 5 cm + 5 cm = 20 cm
(b)8 squares of side 1 cm are cut out of the square D. Now it looks like shape E. What is the length of the boundary of shape E?

Ans. There are 21 edges measuring 1 cm of shape ‘E’.
Hence, the boundary of shape ‘E’ = 1 cm x 21 = 21 cm
(c) The boundary of this is_____+_____+_____+_____ Can we also say that the boundary is 4 x 1 cm?
The boundary of this = 1 cm + 1 cm + 1 cm + 1 cm = 4 cm
So, we can say the boundary of = 4×1 cm.

3. A hockey held is 91 metres 40 cm long and 55 metres wide. How long is the boundary of the held?
Ans. The length of the field = 91 metres 40 cm = 9100 cm + 40 cm
= 9140 cm.
The width of the held = 55 metre = 55 x 100 = 5500 cm Therefore boundary = length + width + length + width
= 9140 + 5500 + 9140 + 5500 .
= 29280 cm
= 29280 -r 100 = 292 metre 80 cm

4. Usha and Valsamma are running a race. Usha is running on the inner circle. Valsamma is running on the outer circle.
Valsamma runs faster than Usha. But still she loses the race. Can you guess why?
Ans. Because inner circle has shorter boundary than outer circle. And Valsamma runs on the outer circle so, she has to cover more length. Because of this she loses the race.

5. Have you seen any race where runners start from different places – like in this picture? Guess why?
Ans. Yes I have seen the race which is organized in such circular track. Such arrangement is made to distances equal.

NCERT Textbook Page 157
School Garden
The students of Class III and IV thought of making a vegetable garden. They chose a place which looked like this.
Students of both the classes thought that garden 1 was bigger. So both wanted to take garden 1. Suddenly Neetu said-I think both gardens are equally big. Quite possible! Let us find out if you are right.
1. How will Neetu find out if the two gardens are equally big?
Ans. Neetu place a long rope along the boundary of garden 1 and measure the length of the boundary. Again Neetu place the rope along with the boundary of garden 2 and measure its length.
Finally, she saw that the length of the boundaries of both gardens are equal. Hence, both the gardens are equal.
